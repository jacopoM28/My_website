Short Bio


The Past

I was raised in Gradisca d’Isonzo, a small town near Trieste, where I was born in 1993. After High School, in 2012, I’ve worked for a year and then I decided to move in Bologna where I got my Bachelor of Science in 2017.

In march 2020 I got my Master degree in “Biodiversity and Evolution”, cum laude, with a dissertation entitled: Molecular Phylogenetics, Divergence time and Evolution of Phasmida (Insecta), supervised by professor Andrea Luchetti. The thesis was the result of one year of intership at the MoZoo Lab, where I was involved in extensive wet lab experiments, followed by phylogentic and comparative analyses, focused on the evolution of wings in Phasmatodea (Insecta) (more info). At the same time, I have worked on two side projects: one focused on phasmids phylogeny and taxonomy with integrative approach between molecular and morphological data - as a first co-author - (more info), and one about taxonomy and phylogeny of the Australian stick insect Candovia - as a collaborator (more info).




The present and the near future

Starting from November 2020 I am a Ph.D student working on molluscs and bivalves evolution in a comparative genomic approach, supervised by professor Fabrizio Ghiselli at the MoZoo Lab. My research lines are mainly two:

  • Characterization and evolution of transposable elements with a focus on horizontal transposons transfer (HTTS).
  • Evolution of Bivalves immuno-system.

Moreover, I am involved in comparative genomics studies of Notostraca
